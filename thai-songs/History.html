History

Early / Pre‑20th Century

  • Traditional folk and court music dominate, region by region.
  • Musical forms tied to ritual, ceremonies, festivals.

Early 20th Century

  • influence begins: Western notation, Western instruments introduced.
  • Phleng Thai sakon: Thai music with Western instrumentation/composition styles; early composers like Eua Sunthornsanan.

Mid‑20th Century

  • Growth of popular genres: luk thung (rural “country-style” music), luk krung (urban music), etc.
  • “Wong shadow” / early rock / pop experiments from the 50s‑60s.

Recent Decades

  • The rise of T‑POP, mainstream pop artists, electronic and hip-hop influences.
  • Fusion, revival movements. Traditional instruments used in modern pop. Experimental electronic artists blending old and new.
  • Social role: how music reflects identity, social issues, regional culture.
